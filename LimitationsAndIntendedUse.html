Ectopy Monitor – Limitations and Intended Use

Last updated: June 17, 2026

Overview

This app does not diagnose heart rhythm disorders. Diagnosis of PVCs, PACs, or any arrhythmia requires an ECG interpreted by a qualified clinician. The app is intended for people who have already been evaluated and have a known history of ectopic beats.

What the app measures

The app analyzes RR intervals from a chest strap heart rate monitor. Certain types of ectopic beats, especially PVCs, create distinctive RR patterns that can be detected reliably without an ECG. These include:

  • premature short RR intervals
  • compensatory pauses
  • repeating bigeminy or trigeminy patterns
  • ectopic couplets and triplets with clear RR signatures

When these patterns are present, RR based detection is strong and consistent.

What the app cannot measure

Some atrial ectopic beats do not produce a detectable change in RR timing. These include:

  • interpolated PACs
  • PACs with concealed conduction
  • P wave only events
  • atrial tachycardias without RR perturbation

These events require ECG morphology and cannot be identified from RR data alone. This is a limitation of all RR based methods, not just this app.

Intended use

This app is designed for monitoring, not diagnosis. It is useful for people who:

  • already know they have PVCs or frequent ectopy
  • want to track burden over time
  • want to see how ectopy changes with sleep, stress, exercise, caffeine, or medication
  • want to identify patterns, triggers, or trends
  • want a lightweight continuous tool that complements, rather than replaces, ECG based evaluation

Clinical positioning

RR based analysis is not a substitute for ECG, but it is a valid and practical method for tracking ectopic burden once the underlying rhythm has been identified. PVCs in particular have a well defined RR signature, which makes them suitable for RR based monitoring.
