Beat Timing Viewer - Limitations and Intended Use

Last updated: June 24, 2026

Overview

Beat Timing Viewer does not diagnose heart conditions. The app analyzes beat-to-beat timing in RR intervals from compatible Bluetooth heart-rate monitors and shows how successive intervals vary compared with a recent timing pattern. Results are informational only and are not medical advice.

Timing labels shown in the app (such as short beats, duple or tuple patterns, or highly irregular timing windows) describe RR timing patterns only. They are not a diagnosis of any specific heart rhythm disorder.

What the app does

  • Presents rolling charts, session summaries, and exportable beat lists from RR-interval data.
  • Works with widely available consumer chest-strap heart-rate monitors that provide RR-interval data.
  • Supports observation of timing and rhythm trends over a session or across saved sessions.
  • Enables collection, saving, and optional export of beat-to-beat timing data on the device.
  • Separates automatic timing classifications from user-entered notes in exported data files.

What the app does not do

  • It is not an ECG/EKG.
  • It is not a Holter monitor or medical device.
  • It does not identify or diagnose any specific heart rhythm disorder or clinical condition.
  • It does not determine clinical significance or provide medical advice.
  • It does not interpret Poincare plots or other visualizations beyond displaying the data.

What RR timing analysis can reflect

When beat-to-beat timing changes in clear, repeating ways, RR-interval analysis can highlight patterns such as:

  • Short beats - RR intervals classified as shorter than the rolling baseline used for comparison.
  • Recurring timing patterns, such as duple or tuple rhythms (roughly every second or third beat).
  • Highly irregular timing windows, where classification may be less reliable.

Session summaries may also show rolling short beat ratio thresholds (for example, ratio above 10%, 15%, or 20% in a rolling window). These are timing statistics only; the app does not interpret their clinical meaning.

What RR timing alone cannot establish

RR-interval data records when beats occur, not the electrical shape of each beat. Not every rhythm change produces a clear or distinguishable change in RR timing alone. These limits apply to RR-based methods in general, not only to this app. Symptoms or clinical concerns warrant consultation with a qualified healthcare professional and appropriate clinical testing as recommended.

Intended use

This app is intended for informational and wellness purposes only. Typical uses include:

  • Observing how beat-to-beat timing varies during everyday activities.
  • Tracking timing patterns over time and comparing saved sessions.
  • Adding session notes and reviewing them alongside exported timing data.
  • Preparing questions or material for discussions with a healthcare provider.

The app is a consumer timing tool that may complement personal logs, session notes, and other records kept outside the app; it does not replace professional evaluation when that is needed.

Exported data

When beat data is shared from the app, exported files include automatic Classification tags and a separate User note column for manually entered text. Classification tags are generated by the app's timing analysis; user notes reflect manual entry only. Both are provided for personal review and record-keeping; neither constitutes a medical report.

When files are exported or shared outside the app, timing and heart-rate data may be visible to others or third-party services. Sharing to trusted destinations remains at the exporter's discretion and risk.
