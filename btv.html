Beat Timing Viewer - Support

Last updated: June 25, 2026

Overview

Beat Timing Viewer is designed to be self-explanatory on the device. Help for charts, sessions, exports, and hardware setup is built into the app as info bubbles (short help text opened from the info control on each screen).

The developer does not operate a separate support desk or email channel. For questions about how the app works, use the in-app info bubbles and the documents linked below.

First launch

On first use, the app shows an Important Notice screen covering intended use, privacy on the device, and a link to the privacy policy. That screen must be accepted before continuing.

Where to find info bubbles

Look for the info control on the screen you are using. Topics covered include:

  • Live timing view - overview of the live charts; rolling RR intervals; rolling short-beat ratio; rolling heart rate; Poincare plot.
  • Heart-rate monitor selection - compatible chest-strap monitors, RR-interval data, and Bluetooth setup notes.
  • Session summary - saving an encrypted session on the device.
  • History - session list and exports; short-beat ratio trend across sessions; exporting beat-level session detail.

Wording in the app may vary slightly by platform (iOS or Android) but covers the same topics.

Common topics

Monitor not connecting or no RR data

Open the info bubble on the monitor selection screen. The app needs a Bluetooth chest-strap heart-rate monitor that sends RR-interval data. Many wrist-based watches do not provide reliable beat-to-beat timing for this app.

Saving and exporting data

Sessions can be saved in encrypted form on the device. Export creates a decrypted file only when export or share is initiated from the app. Info bubbles on the summary and history screens describe what each export contains.

Timing labels and classifications

Automatic timing labels describe beat spacing for personal review. They are not clinical diagnoses. For full scope and limitations, see the document linked below.
